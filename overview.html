When we're going

Late September is the sweet spot

September is the tail of summer easing into autumn. The first half is hot and humid; by the last week it settles into warm, comfortable days with far fewer tourists than spring or peak fall. It's typhoon season, but Tokyo, Kyoto and Osaka sit inland and mostly just see passing rain.

What to expect

Warm and walkable by late month, autumn flavors arriving on menus, and quiet temples and streets. We keep one open day in the middle as weather insurance, skip non-refundable hotels, and pack a light rain jacket instead of an umbrella.

The route

Three cities, three different feelings

We start in the modern rush of Tokyo, slow down in the old capital of Kyoto, and finish loud and full in Osaka — with the bullet train stitching it all together.
